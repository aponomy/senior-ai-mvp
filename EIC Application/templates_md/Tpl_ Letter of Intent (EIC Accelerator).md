# Letter of Intent - Template Guidance

> **For EIC Accelerator Applications**

---

## Overview

Please include all your Letters of Intent **merged into a single PDF document**.

The letters should be:
- As detailed as possible
- Avoid generic/copy-paste "support" letters
- Outline specific commitments
- Avoid unsigned, undated letters
- From relevant parties (customers, users, real investors)

## Essential Elements to Include

### 1. Identification

- Company name, address, logo, and contact details
- Name, title, and signature of authorized representative (CEO, Director, VP)

### 2. Purpose of the Letter

- Clear statement of intent: **investment**, **partnership**, **pilot**, or **purchase**
- The LOI should relate directly to the startup's project or technology

### 3. Nature of Commitment

- Mention of investment amount, purchase volume, or collaboration scope
- Define type of support: **financial**, **technical**, or **commercial**
- Include expected timeline (e.g., "within 12 months after project completion")

### 4. Strategic Relevance

- Explain why the partner is interested in this technology
- Link to EIC Accelerator priorities

### 5. Mutual Benefit

- Short statement on how both sides benefit from the collaboration

### 6. Conditions (if applicable)

- Optional clause like: "Subject to satisfactory validation and funding approval"

### 7. Date, Validity, and Signature

- Date of issue
- Validity period (e.g., 12 months)
- Handwritten or digital signature

---

## Tips

- Be specific about amounts, timelines, and commitments
- Ensure letters are signed and dated
- Include company letterhead
- Focus on concrete commitments, not vague support
