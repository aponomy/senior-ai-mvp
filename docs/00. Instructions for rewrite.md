# Instructions for Major Conceptual Changes & Additions

**Purpose**: Process for systematically investigating and applying major conceptual changes across the entire EIC application when new strategic insights, positioning shifts, or fundamental additions emerge.

**When to Use**: 
- New market positioning discovered (e.g., "AI accessibility layer")
- Major competitive landscape shift (e.g., Adobe Firefly/Perplexity AI suites)
- Fundamental reframing of value proposition (e.g., defensive ‚Üí empowering)
- Strategic pivot affecting multiple sections (technology, excellence, impact, business model)

**Worked Example**: Adding "AI accessibility layer" concept - thousands of AI services (ChatGPT, Midjourney, Adobe Firefly, Perplexity) made accessible through Create/Learn/Connect/Execute framework.

---

## Phase 1: Change Identification & Scope Assessment

### 1. **Capture the Change**
   - [ ] **What is the new concept/change?**
     - Write one-sentence description
     - Example: "Senior AI is an accessibility layer making thousands of existing AI services (creative, learning, exploration + execution) accessible to elderly users"
   
   - [ ] **What triggered this?**
     - User feedback
     - Market research discovery
     - Competitive analysis
     - Strategic realization
   
   - [ ] **Old vs. New positioning**
     - What was the previous framing?
     - Example: "Defensive ('don't get left behind') ‚Üí Empowering ('join AI revolution')"

### 2. **Initial Impact Assessment**
   - [ ] **Quick scan**: Which materials mention the affected concept?
     ```bash
     grep -r "old concept" docs/ "EIC Application/"
     ```
   
   - [ ] **Estimate scope** (check all that apply):
     - [ ] Value proposition / positioning
     - [ ] Competitive landscape
     - [ ] Technology description
     - [ ] Innovation/Excellence claims
     - [ ] Market opportunity / Impact
     - [ ] Business model
     - [ ] Risk assessment
     - [ ] Team/Partners requirements
     - [ ] Pitch deck slides
     - [ ] Core documentation (README, Description)

### 3. **Strategic Clarification Questions**
   - [ ] **STOP - Ask user 3-5 SHORT questions** (one sentence each):
     - Scope: "Should this apply to entire application or specific sections?"
     - Depth: "Replace existing positioning or add alongside?"
     - Priorities: "Which documents are most critical to update first?"
     - Creative examples: "Any specific AI tools/services to highlight?"
     - Evidence needs: "Do we have/need research backing this?"
   
   - [ ] **WAIT for answers** before proceeding

## Phase 2: Deep Investigation

### 4. **Competitive & Market Research**
   - [ ] **Identify new comparables**
     - Who else operates in this repositioned space?
     - Example: Adobe Firefly (creative AI suite), Perplexity (research/learn), ChatGPT Plus
   
   - [ ] **Use expert for market analysis**
     ```markdown
     Question: "What is the competitive landscape for AI accessibility layers 
     or AI suite platforms (like Adobe Firefly, Perplexity) that bundle multiple 
     AI services? Focus on Europe, elderly market, 2025 onwards."
     
     Context: Senior AI repositioned as accessibility layer making thousands of 
     AI tools accessible to 65+ Europeans. Need to understand: competitors, 
     differentiation, market gaps, positioning strategy.
     
     Reasoning effort: high
     ```
   
   - [ ] **Save research**: `docs/ask_expert/[issue]_[task]_[topic]-competitive-2025-[date].md`

### 5. **Technology & Innovation Implications**
   - [ ] **Architecture/tech stack changes?**
     - Does this require different technical approach?
     - New integrations needed? (API connections to other AI services)
   
   - [ ] **Use expert for innovation assessment**
     ```markdown
     Question: "What are the technical innovation challenges for creating an 
     'AI accessibility layer' that makes existing AI services (ChatGPT, Midjourney, 
     Adobe Firefly) accessible to elderly users with cognitive decline?"
     
     Context: [Include key technical details, constraints, standards like W3C/ISO 21801]
     
     Reasoning effort: high
     ```
   
   - [ ] **Save research**: `docs/ask_expert/[issue]_[task]_Innovation-implications-2025-[date].md`

### 6. **Evidence & Research Backing**
   - [ ] **Search existing research**
     ```bash
     grep -r "cognitive|accessibility|elderly|HCI" docs/ask_expert/
     ```
   
   - [ ] **Identify research gaps**
     - Do we have evidence for new claims?
     - W3C standards coverage?
     - User studies supporting this approach?
   
   - [ ] **Commission new research if needed**
     - Use expert to find academic backing
     - Save to `docs/ask_expert/[issue]_[task]_Research-backing-2025-[date].md`

### 7. **Business Model & Impact Assessment**
   - [ ] **Does this change revenue model?**
     - New cost structure? (API fees for third-party services)
     - Different pricing tiers?
     - Partnership requirements?
   
   - [ ] **Impact scope changes?**
     - Different user benefits? (creative empowerment vs. essential access)
     - New impact metrics needed?
     - Social innovation angle shift?
   
   - [ ] **Use expert if major business implications**
   - [ ] **Save research**: `docs/ask_expert/[issue]_[task]_Business-implications-2025-[date].md`

### 8. **Create Analysis Document**
   - [ ] **Synthesize findings**: `docs/reasoning/[issue]_[task]_[change-name]-analysis.md`
     - What we learned from research
     - Implications for each affected section
     - Prioritized update list
     - Consistency challenges
     - New evidence/claims to add
   
   - [ ] **Example structure**:
     ```markdown
     # AI Accessibility Layer - Change Analysis
     
     ## The Change
     [One-paragraph summary]
     
     ## Research Findings
     - Competitive landscape: ...
     - Technical implications: ...
     - Evidence available: ...
     
     ## Affected Materials (Priority Order)
     1. Pitch deck: Slides 1-5 (value prop)
     2. Core docs: README, Description
     3. Application drafts: Excellence, Impact
     4. Synthesis docs: Innovation-Breakthrough
     
     ## Update Strategy
     [Section-by-section plan]
     
     ## Consistency Checks
     [Old terminology to find/replace]
     ```

## Phase 3: Systematic Updates

### 9. **Priority 1: Pitch Deck**
   - [ ] **Identify affected slides**
     - Usually: Title, Problem, Solution, Innovation, Market/Impact
   
   - [ ] **Update with new positioning**
     - Use `replace_string_in_file` for surgical edits
     - Maintain slide length constraints (30-60 lines)
     - Keep visual hierarchy for elderly users
   
   - [ ] **Example affected areas**:
     - Titles/subtitles
     - Problem framing
     - Solution description
     - Competitive differentiation
     - Innovation claims
     - Market opportunity sizing

### 10. **Priority 2: Core Documentation**
   - [ ] **Update docs/01. Description.md**
     - Project title/subtitle
     - Value proposition paragraph
     - Core features/framework
     - Competitive positioning
   
   - [ ] **Update README.md**
     - Project description
     - Key differentiators
     - Example use cases
   
   - [ ] **Verify consistency**
     ```bash
     grep -E "old concept|previous positioning" docs/01*.md README.md
     ```

### 11. **Priority 3: Application Draft Sections**
   - [ ] **Excellence section** (`EIC Application/application_draft/`)
     - Technology description
     - Innovation breakthroughs
     - Research foundations
     - Competitive advantage
   
   - [ ] **Impact section**
     - User benefits (align with new positioning)
     - Social innovation angle
     - Market opportunity
     - Scalability narrative
   
   - [ ] **Implementation section**
     - Technical architecture (if affected)
     - Partnership strategy (if new integrations)
     - Risk assessment (new competitive risks)
   
   - [ ] **Work & Resources**
     - Team needs (new skills for AI integrations?)
     - Budget allocation (API costs?)

### 12. **Priority 4: Synthesis & Research Documents**
   - [ ] **Update existing synthesis docs** (`docs/synthesis/`)
     - Incorporate new research
     - Align conclusions with new positioning
     - Add new competitive insights
   
   - [ ] **Create new synthesis if needed**
     - For major additions, may need dedicated synthesis
     - Example: `10_01_AI-Accessibility-Layer-synthesis.md`

### 13. **Cross-Reference Check**
   - [ ] **Search for old terminology**
     ```bash
     grep -r "old concept" "EIC Application/" docs/
     ```
   
   - [ ] **Check for contradictions**
     - Are there sections that still reflect old positioning?
     - Do all competitive comparisons align?
     - Are impact claims consistent with new framing?
   
   - [ ] **Verify evidence alignment**
     - Does research cited support new claims?
     - Are standards/citations still relevant?

## Phase 4: Quality Assurance

### 14. **Consistency Audit**
   - [ ] **Create checklist of key terms/concepts**
     ```markdown
     Old ‚Üí New:
     - "Safe tool for banking" ‚Üí "Gateway to AI ecosystem (create, learn, explore + execute)"
     - "Don't get left behind" ‚Üí "Join AI revolution, empowered participation"
     - "Simple interface for elderly" ‚Üí "Accessibility layer making 1000+ AI services accessible"
     - Competitor: "Other elderly apps" ‚Üí "Adobe Firefly, Perplexity, ChatGPT Plus (but not accessible)"
     ```
   
   - [ ] **Scan all files for old terminology**
   - [ ] **Replace systematically**

### 15. **EIC Evaluation Criteria Check**
   - [ ] **Excellence**: Does new positioning strengthen innovation claims?
   - [ ] **Impact**: Does new framing enhance social/economic impact?
   - [ ] **Implementation**: Are new risks/partnerships addressed?
   - [ ] **Competitive advantage**: Is differentiation clearer?

### 16. **User Preference Alignment**
   - [ ] **Check against user's stated preferences**
     - Example: User liked "14+ life tools" in Create/Learn/Connect/Execute framework
     - Ensure creative examples included (not just utilitarian)
     - Verify empowerment framing (not defensive)

### 17. **Document the Change**
   - [ ] **Create change log**: `docs/decisions/[date]_[change-name]-rewrite.md`

     - Application: [list sections]
