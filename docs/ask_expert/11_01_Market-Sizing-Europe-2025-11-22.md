### 2. Digital Exclusion & Tech Adoption Among 65+

| Metric | 65-74 Age | 75+ Age |

| **Internet use** (last 3 months) | 65-70% | 35-45% |

**Country Variance**:
- **High performers** (Nordics, UK, Netherlands): 65-74 smartphone ~85-90%, internet 85%+

**Sources**:
- Eurostat ICT usage in households (isoc_ci_ifp_iu)

|-------------|-----------|---------|
| **At least "basic" digital skills** | 25-30% | 10-15% |
| **Lacking basic digital skills** | **70-75%** | **85-90%** |

**Applied to 109-110M aged 65+**: **70-85 million lack basic digital skills**

**Sources**:
- Eurostat DESI indicators (isoc_sk_dskl_i)
- DESI 2023/2024 country reports

#### Multi-Step Task Performance

| Task Type | 65-74 Age | 75+ Age |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| **Online banking** (past 3 months) | 40-50% | 15-25% |
| **eGovernment** interactions | 30-40% | 15-20% |

**Pain Points**:
- Multi-step flows (login + 2FA + navigation)
- Healthcare portals
- Government forms
- Low DESI scores correlate to avoidance/anxiety

**Nordic Exception**: 
- 65-74 online banking >80% (Sweden, Denmark, Finland, Norway)
- Yet still below younger cohorts - "tech-curious but struggling" segment exists even in high-adoption markets

**Sources**:
- Eurostat eBanking (isoc_bdek_ufin)
- Eurostat eGovernment (isoc_bde15ei)
- European Commission DESI 2023 country reports
- UK Ofcom "Digital Exclusion 2024"

#### "Digitally Excluded" Population

| Category | Percentage | Population (EU27+UK+NO) |
|----------|-----------|------------------------|
| **Never used internet** | 35-45% of 65+ | **38-45 million** |
| **Lacking basic digital skills** | 70-75% of 65+ | **70-85 million** |

**Critical Insight**: The opportunity is NOT device provision but **simplifying multi-step tasks and providing trustworthy assistance** for people who have devices but lack confidence/skills.

### 3. Cognitive Challenge Populations (Secondary Market)

#### Dementia

- **Current prevalence**: 10-11 million across EU27 + UK + Norway
- **Age overlap**: Predominantly 75+
- **Growth trajectory**: Substantial increase expected by 2050 (Alzheimer Europe projections)

**Sources**:
- Alzheimer Europe "Dementia in Europe Yearbooks" (2019, 2021)
- ADI World Alzheimer Report 2021

#### Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI)

- **Prevalence**: 12-18% of adults 60+
- **EU27 + UK + Norway**: **13-20 million with MCI**
- **Age overlap**: Significant overlap with 65+ population

**Sources**:
- Petersen RC et al., JAMA/Neurology reviews
- European Brain Council reports

#### ADHD in Adults

- **Adult prevalence**: 2-3% overall
- **65+ prevalence**: ~1-2% (underdiagnosed, remission, comorbidity with cognitive complaints)
- **Characteristics**: Persistent attention/executive function challenges

**Sources**:
- Fayyad et al., World Psychiatry 2017
- Kooij et al., European consensus guidelines

#### Intellectual/Learning Disabilities

- **General population prevalence**: ~1%
- **Digital access**: Amplified barriers for older adults with ID

**Sources**:
- Maulik et al., Lancet 2011
- EU Agency for Fundamental Rights disability inclusion reports

#### Market Overlap Insight

**Nearly all dementia and most MCI are in 65+ population.** Digital assistance tailored for cognitive load reduction (visual cues, branching, fewer steps) addresses **both primary (elderly) and secondary (cognitive challenge) segments simultaneously**.

### 4. Market Segmentation (Behavioral Lens for Senior AI)

#### Three Primary Segments

**A. Tech-Curious Elderly** (Have devices, want to use, struggle with complexity)
- **Size**: **30-40 million**
- **Calculation**: Device owners among 65+ (~55-60M) × lacking basic skills (~60-70%)
- **Characteristics**: 
  - Own smartphone/tablet/computer
  - Want to stay connected, manage own affairs
  - Frustrated by complexity, fear making mistakes
  - Will pay for confidence and independence

**B. Digitally Dependent** (Must use online services but anxious)
- **Size**: **20-30 million**
- **Calculation**: Internet users using e-banking/e-government occasionally (~25-35M) minus high-skill users
- **Characteristics**:
  - Required to use digital services (banking, healthcare, government)
  - High anxiety, rely on family/caregivers for help
  - Will pay to reduce dependence on others
  - Fear scams and mistakes

**C. Care-Supported** (Family/caregivers seeking solutions for loved ones)
- **Size**: Tens of millions (50+ million informal carers in EU, many supporting 65+)
- **Characteristics**:
  - Adult children seeking peace of mind
  - Caregivers wanting to reduce support burden
  - Often the actual payer even when not the user
  - Value safety, monitoring, independence for loved one

**Segment Overlap**: These segments overlap significantly. Together they frame the core TAM for a simplified, task-orchestrating AI assistant.

### 5. Willingness to Pay (€10-20/month Validation)

#### Telecare/Personal Alarm Benchmarks (Closest EU Analogue)

| Country | Service Type | Monthly Cost | Adoption |
|---------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| **UK** | Pendant alarms (Careline, AgeUK) | £15-£25 (~€18-€30) | Widespread municipal/family purchase |
| **Netherlands** | Zorgalarm | €19-€30 | Standard offering |
| **Sweden** | Trygghetslarm (municipal) | SEK 200-300 (~€18-€28) | Municipal provision, copay |
| **Norway** | Trygghetslarm | NOK 300-400 (~€25-€35) | Municipal, partial subsidy |
| **France** | Téléassistance | €20-€30 | Often reimbursed by departments/mutuelles |
| **Germany** | Hausnotruf | €20-€30 | Covered by Pflegeversicherung in some cases |

**Key Insight**: Families and municipalities **routinely pay €15-35/month** for safety/digital help services. A €10-20/month AI assistant sits **within an established mental model and budget line**.

**Sources**: 
- National/municipal telecare tariffs
- Operator public price lists (Careline UK, Zorgalarm NL, municipal fee schedules 2022-2024)

#### Communications/Entertainment Comparators

- **Seniors' mobile plans**: €10-€30/month (MVNO and MNO senior tariffs)
- **Pay-TV basic packages**: €20-€40/month in Western Europe
- **Household communications spend**: Stable 2-4% share of consumption (Eurostat Household Budget Survey)

**Implication**: €10-20/month digital assistant falls within **existing subscription behavior patterns**.

#### Caregiver Willingness to Pay

- **UK and EU caregiver surveys**: 25-40% willing to pay £/€10-25/month for remote monitoring/alerts
- **Value drivers**: Reduced worry, time savings, peace of mind

**Sources**:
- Carers UK tech-enabled care surveys
- Local authority pilot evaluations
- AAL (Active & Assisted Living) programme studies

## Key Insights for EIC Application

### 1. Demographic Urgency
- **Fastest-growing cohort** (85+) has **lowest digital skills** - market need accelerating
- 85+ grows 45% by 2035 while digital service requirements increase
- **Europe-specific aging crisis** creates regulatory/policy tailwinds

### 2. Large, Underserved Market
- **70-85 million Europeans** have devices but lack skills to use them effectively
- Existing solutions focus on either companionship OR single-domain tasks, not both
- **Gap**: Broad-domain, cognitively-accessible assistant with secure task execution

### 3. Proven Willingness to Pay
- €15-35/month telecare services widely adopted
- €10-20/month sits **within established mental models**
- Both B2C (family pays) and B2B2C (payer/municipality) validated payment channels

### 4. Strong B2B2C Distribution Potential
- 50-60k care facilities
- 30k+ home care providers
- 100+ major payers actively seeking digital aging solutions
- 10-15 telco groups needing senior segment differentiation

### 5. Accessible Pricing Strategy Validated
- ElliQ ($60/mo) and premium solutions leave large price-sensitive market unserved
- Senior mobile plans, TV, and telecare show €10-20 is **comfortable spend band**
- **Democratizes access** while maintaining sustainability at scale

### 6. European Added Value
- Data sovereignty concerns favor EU-based solution
- GDPR compliance built-in vs US/Asian alternatives
- Multilingual expansion critical (English, Swedish, Norwegian, Danish, German, Dutch, French)
- Aligns with EU Digital Decade, Accessibility Act, Healthy Ageing priorities
